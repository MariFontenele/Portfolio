Amazon rainforest

Combining many sources of data to find which factors have greater impact on deforestation throughout the years 2008 to 2019

Deforestation data

The data examined in this study is from Terrabrasilis — Plataforma de dados geográficos — INPE. It is the Brazilian government organization that supervise the Amazon rainforest area. Here, the focus is going to be on deforestation data throughout the years 2008 to 2019

Counties: the ground for analysis

The first step is to situate the geospacial deforestation data into the basis for our analysis: Legal Amazon counties. All other sources of data are going to be on this same frame. A geopandas spatial join function is used to find the placement of each deforestation focus

Target variable

The variable we are interested in is deforestation. More specifically the cumulative deforestation from 2008 to 2019 Since ~20% of Counties doesn’t have any deforestation area, the rest of the Counties with deforestation (~80%) are going to be divided into quartiles

Therefore, the data is divided into quintiles: high-high, high-low, low-high, low-low and zero

However, the goal is to train the model to predict only the high-high class, so it is going to be set to True (or 1) and all the other classes will be set to False (or 0)

Predictive features

Now it’s time to gather other sources of data that potentially have impact on deforestation. Three categories are going to be considered:

  • Rural Industry: the Agriculture (acres cultivated) and Cattle farming (bovine head) per county
  • Social & Economic: estimated GDP per county and Human Development Index metrics — HDI, Income HDI, Education HDI, Longevity HDI
  • Legal protected areas: spatial data with indigenous areas to show if the deforestation is happening there. The counties will have a True or False classification that demonstrates if any proportion of deforestation of that particular county is happening in protected reserves

In harmony with the cumulative deforestation target, the Agriculture, Cattle and GDP data throughout the years of 2008 to 2019 area also going to be cumulative of all period considered. Only the Human Development Indexes of counties are going to be constant

Exploratory data analysis

The EDA of the study is divided into two parts: i) a quick univariate and multivariate descriptive analysis and ii) hypothesis driven analysis. Only the highlights are going to be shared here since it was an extensive investigation

Pearson correlation

The correlation analysis is important to get a feeling about which features are later going to be considered relevant by the algorithms

cattle_acc: cumulative cattle demonstrated an impressive positive correlation of 0.57 with cumulative deforestation while the agriculture cumulative data indicated only 0.015 correlation; this relevance will be further confirmed by the Machine Learning algorithms

area_countie: has a positive correlation of 0.52. It makes sense that big areas have bigger cumulative deforestation since there are more area to deforest; once we are aiming to predict external and not intrinsic characteristics, this feature will not be in the model. However, it is interesting that cumulative cattle has a correlation bigger even than the correlation with the area of the counties which is naturally high

HDI_educacao: it is also intriguing that Education Human Development Index demonstrated a negative correlation with cumulative deforestation of -0.14. This relevance will also be confirmed with the Machine Learning Modelling

Hypothesis driven analysis

In harmony with the target variable classification, the framework of this analysis is also based on quintiles. The goal is to find out if the top 20% of others sources of data coincided with the top 20% cumulative deforestation counties (target)

Here it’s a summary of all the hypothesis considered. However, in this article only the highlight findings will be detailed

Rural Industry: agriculture x cattle

In line with the correlation analysis, the top quintile of cumulative deforestation and cumulative cattle share ~52% of counties. It is worth seeing if the Machine Learning algorithms will also consider Cattle farming a great predictor for cumulative deforestation

On the other hand, the top quintile of agriculture and cumulative deforestation share ~38% of counties

Social & Economic: GDP and Education HDI

Counties in the top quintile of cumulative Gross Domestic Product (GDP) share ~40% of counties with the top cumulative deforestation quintile

Since the correlation analysis reveled a negative correlation, the lowest quintile of Education Human Development Index (HDI) and the top quintile of cumulative deforestation share nearly ~1/3 of counties

Machine Learning modelling overview

The type of Machine Learning algorithm used here is Classification since this is a prediction of the high-high cumulative deforestation class. Specifically, this four algorithms will be trained on the dataset:

Random Forest: it is a tree based model built with multiple ensemble decision trees created with the bagging method. Then, all the classifiers take a weighted vote on their predictions. Since the algorithm goal is not trying to find a linear function to describe the event, it works for problems with more complex behaviour

Extremely Randomized Trees: it works on the same logic of Random Forest, but instead of using multiple ensemble bootstrap replicas, it uses the original sample. The randomization is added on the cut point to splitting the tree nodes, while the previous algorithm chooses the best optimum split, the Extremely Randomized Trees chooses it randomly

XGBoost: it is also a tree based model but they are built in a different way. While Random Forests builds each tree independently, XGBoost builds one tree at the time learning with its predecessor. Therefore, this algorithm doesn’t combine results at the end of the process by taking majority votes, it combines the results along the way

CatBoost: this model share the same logic of XGBoost, however the focus is in optimizing the decision trees for categorical variables. The goal is to work better with features with classes that have relation with one another like, for example, different colors

Performance comparison

In the Class Prediction Error report we can see that the algorithms performed almost the same, but CatBoost behaved slightly worse in this dataset

Comparing the ROC AUC curve and the Precision-Recall curve they performed virtually the same

However, the mean Cross Validation score of CatBoost was not as good as the others

With all this in mind, here I’ll consider that all algorithms performed virtually the same except CatBoost that had a lower efficiency. Therefore, CatBoost is not going to be part of the feature importance analysis

Feature importance analysis

For this study, this is the most meaningful analysis since the goal is to find out which features the Machine Learning algorithms are considering more important for predicting the high-high cumulative deforestation class. Features with ‘_acc’ endings are the cumulative variables and ‘_class’ are variables with the quintile classification from 0 to 4

In conformity with the previous Exploratory Data Analysis, Cattle farming is, overall, the most predicting feature for cumulative deforestation in this period of time

Conclusion

In conformity with the previous correlation analysis, Cattle farming is, overall, the most predicting feature for cumulative deforestation in this period of time

GDP of the counties is the second factor that the algorithms considered relevant for predicting high-high deforestation class. Here it’s also an illustrational map of both variables

In third place, Education Human Development Index reveled an interesting negative correlation with counties that had the biggest deforestation areas during this time

The fourth place is Agriculture that also exhibited a positive correlation with counties with greater deforestation; however, this relathionship is not as significant as the Cattle farming

It is also worth mentioning that even though it’s not as relevant for prediction, counties that have deforestation in indigenous areas, classification equals to True or 1, demonstrated a positive correlation with high-high deforestation
